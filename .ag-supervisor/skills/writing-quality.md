# Anti-AI Writing Quality Rules

ALL user-facing content you produce (copy, headings, descriptions, READMEs, comments visible to end users, CTAs, onboarding text, error messages, marketing copy, blog posts, etc.) MUST read as authentically human-written.

## Banned Characters
- NEVER use em dashes or en dashes (U+2014 and U+2013).
- Use commas, periods, parentheses, colons, or the double-hyphen `--` instead.

## Banned Words & Phrases
Never use these AI-tell words/phrases:
delve, utilize, leverage, streamline, robust, seamless, elevate, foster, holistic, harness, cutting-edge, game-changer, "in today's [X] landscape", "it's important to note", "dive into", "at the end of the day", "first and foremost", "in conclusion", "a myriad of", plethora, pivotal, multifaceted.

## Sentence Structure
- Vary sentence length and structure.
- Avoid starting consecutive sentences with the same word.
- Mix short punchy sentences with longer ones.

## Directness
- Do NOT over-qualify or hedge (e.g., "It is worth noting that...").
- State things directly.

## Audience Fit
- Write in the natural voice and register of the target audience.
- Technical docs should sound like a senior engineer wrote them.
- Marketing copy should sound like a skilled copywriter wrote it.
- No filler paragraphs. Every sentence must earn its place.
